GNS101 – Use of English

1. Communication

Communication is the process of sending and receiving information from one person to another.

Importance: It helps in learning, sharing ideas and building relationships.

2. Elements of Communication
ElementMeaning
SenderPerson who sends the message
MessageInformation being sent
ChannelMedium used
ReceiverPerson who receives the message
FeedbackResponse from the receiver
3. Types of Communication
  • Verbal Communication – spoken or written words.
  • Non-Verbal Communication – gestures, facial expressions and body language.
4. Effective Listening

Good listening improves understanding.

Listening skills:

  • Pay attention
  • Avoid distractions
  • Take notes
  • Ask questions
5. Reading Skills

Reading is the ability to understand written information.

Types of reading:

  • Skimming
  • Scanning
  • Intensive Reading
  • Extensive Reading
6. Vocabulary Development

Vocabulary refers to the words known and used by a person.

Ways to improve vocabulary:

  • Read regularly
  • Use a dictionary
  • Learn synonyms and antonyms
7. Sentence Structure

A sentence is a group of words that expresses a complete thought.

Parts of a sentence: Subject and Predicate.

8. Types of Sentences
TypeExample
DeclarativeThe class begins at 8 a.m.
InterrogativeWhat is your name?
ImperativeClose the door.
ExclamatoryWhat a beautiful day!
9. Paragraph Development

A good paragraph contains:

  • Topic sentence
  • Supporting details
  • Concluding sentence
10. Comprehension and Summary Writing

Comprehension tests understanding of a passage.

Summary writing presents the main ideas in a shorter form.

Steps in summary writing:

  1. Read the passage carefully
  2. Identify key points
  3. Write briefly in your own words
11. Formal and Informal Letter Writing

Formal Letter: Used for official communication.

Informal Letter: Used for friends and family.

Parts of a formal letter: Address, Date, Salutation, Body, Closing and Signature.

12. Essay Writing

An essay is a piece of writing on a particular topic.

Structure of an essay:

  1. Introduction
  2. Body
  3. Conclusion

Example Topic: "The Importance of Education"
