GNS127 – Citizenship Education

1. Meaning of Citizenship

Citizenship is the legal relationship between an individual and a country.

Types of citizenship:

  • By birth
  • By registration
  • By naturalization
2. Fundamental Human Rights
RightMeaning
Right to lifeProtection of human life
Freedom of speechExpression of opinions
Freedom of movementTravel within the country
Freedom of religionChoice of worship
3. Duties and Responsibilities of Citizens
  • Obey the law
  • Pay taxes
  • Respect national symbols
  • Protect public property
  • Participate in community development
4. Democracy

Democracy is a system of government in which power belongs to the people.

Features:

  • Free and fair elections
  • Majority rule
  • Protection of minority rights
  • Rule of law
5. Rule of Law

The rule of law means that everyone is equal before the law.

Principles:

  • Supremacy of the law
  • Equality before the law
  • Fair hearing
6. The Constitution

A constitution is the supreme law of a country.

Functions:

  • Defines powers of government
  • Protects citizens' rights
  • Provides laws and regulations
7. National Symbols

National symbols of Nigeria include:

  • National Flag
  • National Anthem
  • National Pledge
  • Coat of Arms

The green color on the flag represents agriculture, while the white color represents peace.

8. Good Governance

Good governance refers to the proper management of public affairs.

Characteristics:

  • Transparency
  • Accountability
  • Justice
  • Efficiency
9. National Unity and Peace

National unity means people from different ethnic, religious and cultural backgrounds living together peacefully.

Ways to promote unity:

  • Tolerance
  • Respect for others
  • Dialogue
  • Cooperation
10. Civic Participation

Citizens can participate in national development through:

  • Voting during elections
  • Community service
  • Environmental sanitation
  • Public awareness campaigns
